As a result, I couldn't share my enthusiasm with any of my friends or family, and grew frustrated at the difficulty of making something that was useful to non-programmers.


Learning to program in PHP and Javascript changed all that--here was an inherently visual way of writing instantly gratifying programs that I could share with anyone who knows how to internet. I felt empowered to solve the problems I had heretofore only been able to complain about (see Spreadsheet and IPA). Anything I could visualize I could create. Keeping myself from spending all my time programming quickly became much more difficult than the programming itself. Learning new technologies and paradigms became a pleasure and a relief (and in the case of jQuery, a suspicion that I was dabbling in witchcraft).
